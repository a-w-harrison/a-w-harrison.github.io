About Me

Anthony W. Harrison, PhD

I am a clinical psychologist, currently working in The Tic Service at Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children (GOSH). In this role, I assess, diagnose and treat tic conditions (e.g., Tourette syndrome). As part of my work within this specialist service, I also provide consultation to schools and local services regarding management of the challenges associated with these conditions. I have previously worked within tertiary referral services specialising in PTSD, OCD and anxiety, as well as child and adolescent mental health.

I recently completed a PhD at the University of New South Wales, which examined the neurophysiological mechanisms that distinguish self-generated sensations from those created externally. In particular, my research explored the ways in which prediction and attention contribute to the generation of movement, and the processing of resultant stimuli. I worked as a casual academic at UNSW while completing my studies and am pleased to share these recent student evaluations of my tutorials.
