About Me

Anthony W. Harrison

I am currently completing a combined PhD / Masters of Psychology (Clinical) at the University of New South Wales. Under the supervision of Professor Thomas Whitford, my research examines neural markers of the brain's capacity to distinguish sensations that have been self-generated from those created externally.

In addition to research, I am enthusiastic to share my interest in perception, cognition, statistics and psychopathology through teaching. I've worked as a casual academic at UNSW while completing my studies and am pleased to provide access to these recent student evaluations of my tutorials.

I'm also a practising psychologist, with experience treating a range of severe mental disorders. My clinical experience has included work at tertiary referral services specialising in OCD and anxiety, as well as child and adolescent mental health.
