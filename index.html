About Me

Anthony W. Harrison

I am a PhD Candidate and Clinical Psychology Registrar at the University of New South Wales. Under the supervision of Professor Thomas Whitford, my research examines neural markers of the brain's capacity to distinguish sensations that have been self-generated from those created externally. I'm interested in how deficits in these mechanisms contribute to the symptoms of schizophrenia, as well as the cognitive psychopathology of psychosis more generally.

In addition to research, I am enthusiastic to share my interest in perception, cognition, statistics and psychopathology through teaching. I've worked as a casual academic at UNSW while completing my studies and am pleased to share these recent student evaluations of my tutorials.

I'm also a practising psychologist, with experience treating a range of mental health disorders. My clinical experience has included work at tertiary referral services specialising in OCD, PTSD and anxiety, as well as child and adolescent mental health.
